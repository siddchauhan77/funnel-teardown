Brand Overview Section 1
Who is this, and who are they trying to sell to?
This is the AI's ID card for the brand. It shows the company name, website, founder (if found), a one-line description, and their primary ICP — Ideal Customer Profile. That last one is just a fancy way of saying: "who is the perfect person they're trying to reach?"

The confidence badge (high / medium / low) tells you how certain the AI was about what it found. High = it found clear public data. Low = it had to make educated guesses.
📡
Awareness Coverage Map Section 2
Which stages of the customer journey does this brand actually show up in?
Think of buying something as a journey: first you don't know the brand exists → then you realise you have a problem → then you discover their solution → then you're ready to buy. This map shows which of those stages they're actively reaching people at.

Gaps are the money. If a stage is grayed out, that's a hole in their funnel — people at that stage are falling through without being caught.
Unaware Random people who've never heard of them
Problem Aware People who know they have a problem but don't know this brand
Solution Aware People actively looking for a solution like this
Product Aware People who know about this specific brand
Most Aware People ready to buy right now
Customer People who already bought
Advocate Customers who actively refer others

Value Ladder Section 3 · Brunson framework
All the things they sell, from free to most expensive — and how they get you to climb the stairs.
Russell Brunson (ClickFunnels founder) figured out that great businesses don't just sell one thing — they have a staircase of offers. You start with something free, they earn your trust, then they offer something small, then bigger, then their most expensive thing. Each rung makes the next one feel like a natural next step.
◈ Free YouTube videos, podcasts, free books — zero cost, builds trust
◈ Entry Low-ticket offers $7–$97 — books, mini-courses, a small win
◈ Core Main product $97–$2,000 — the thing they're really known for
◈ High-Ticket $2,000+ — masterminds, live events, coaching, equity deals
◈ Continuity Monthly recurring — memberships, subscriptions, retainers
The Ascension Path sentence below the rung bar is the single-sentence version of the full customer journey from free all the way to their biggest offer.
⟶
Funnel Journey Diagram Section 4
A visual map of the path a stranger takes to become a paying customer.
Each box is a step in the funnel. The arrows show where people go next. The colour of each box matches the awareness stage — so you can see at a glance when the funnel is moving people from "never heard of you" toward "ready to buy."

Solid border = the AI actually observed this step (it exists for real). Dashed / ◦ marker = inferred — the AI is pretty sure it exists based on how similar funnels work, but couldn't confirm it directly.
🔍
Journey Step Analysis Section 5 · The main event
For every step in the funnel: what's smart about it, what's missing, and how the messaging works.
This is the deepest part of the report. Each funnel step gets its own card with four things:
🪝 Hook What grabs your attention and makes you stop scrolling. Could be a bold claim, a painful question, or a surprising stat.
📖 Story What belief they're trying to change in your head. The emotional bridge — a founder story, a case study, social proof, or a reframe.
⚡ Offer / CTA What they're asking you to do next. Subscribe, buy the book, book a call, attend the event.
✓ Working Specific tactics that are done well — named with the psychological principle behind why they work.
⚡ Missing Specific gaps — with the conversion cost of the gap and a concrete fix suggestion.
The ◈ rung badge (Free / Entry / Core / High-Ticket / Continuity) in each step header tells you which level of the value ladder this step belongs to.
💰
Offers Section 6
Every product or service this brand sells, with price and what they promise.
Each card is one offer. The headline or promise is the core value statement — what the customer gets or feels. Price shows as "—" if it wasn't publicly listed (common for high-ticket offers that require a call). The awareness level badge shows what stage of buyer this offer is designed for.

What to look for: Is there an entry offer (something cheap to get people in the door)? Is there a gap between free content and the main paid offer? A missing tripwire is one of the most common funnel holes.

Funnel Recap Section 7
The two lists you actually want to screenshot and save.
Worth Stealing (✦) — the top 3–5 tactics from this funnel that any brand could copy. These are the moves that are working so well they deserve to be replicated.

Learning Opportunities (⚠) — the top 3–5 gaps or mistakes in this funnel worth avoiding. If you're building your own funnel, these are the things not to skip.
❓
Open Questions Section 8
The stuff the AI couldn't figure out from public info — these are your research leads.
Email open rates, backend upsell sequences, private community pricing, exact ad spend — this stuff isn't public. The open questions section lists everything the AI had to leave blank. Treat each one as a research prompt: "What would I need to find out to complete this picture?" You can often answer them by actually going through the funnel yourself.
